Projects

2020

Bayesian classification of multivariate time series using vine copula (joint work with Prof. Claudia Czado)

We introduce a vine copula based approach to analyze multivariate time series exhibiting complex dependence structures as well as nonstationary behavior dependence, and employ Bayesian classifier to perform classification. This approach will be applied to time series derived from a neural activity experiment to classify the opening and closing of the eyes. The performance to other classification methods will be investigated.

Dependence Modeling and Bayesian Reliability Analysis for Competing Risks Data

Dependent competing risks model is widespread in reliability analysis of products or systems. It is of importance to capture the dependence information of failure causes. We apply vine copula to model the complex dependence relationship and assess the reliability based on Bayesian framework.
